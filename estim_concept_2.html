What is Premod?

A definition of Premodulated (Premod) current, its internal electronic mixing mechanism, and its clinical advantages for small, isolated treatment zones.

Clinical Context

Premodulated (Premod) therapy is a bipolar electrical stimulation modality designed to treat narrow or isolated areas of the body where placing four electrodes is impossible. Like IFC, Premod is used for pain management and muscle relaxation.

How it works: Pre-Mixed Current

Premod mixes the frequencies inside the device before being applied to the patient. The machine blends a medium-frequency with a lower modulating frequency to create a single current through a single channel consisting of two electrodes. Since the current is pre-mixed, it does not rely on a geometric intersection to function. Requiring only a single channel and two pads, it is useful for targeting small joints, isolated muscle groups, and sensory nerves for localized pain reduction.
