How do I set up Premod?

How to configure a bipolar, two-electrode Premod treatment sequence for targeted, small surface muscle or nerve stimulation.

This procedure is used when a patient requires localized pain relief or targeted muscle stimulation in a narrow, restricted, or small anatomical area. You will perform this task instead of an IFC setup when treating smaller joints and limbs—such as the wrist, ankle, fingers, or forearm muscle—where a four-electrode quadripolar pattern cannot physically fit on the skin.
  1. Place the two electrodes directly over the target muscle or on either side of the isolated joint space.
  2. Plug both electrode lead wires into a single output port on the machine (e.g., Channel 1 only).
  3. On the display home menu, select Electrotherapy .
  4. Then, select Premod .
  5. Select Treatment Time and use console arrow keys to achieve the desired time.
  6. Press to finalize time.
  7. Slowly rotate the main intensity dial clockwise. Instruct the patient to inform you when they feel a strong, comfortable "tingling."
  8. Press START .
The active channel indicator will show a steady output stream on the console. The patient will report a localized, rhythmic tingling sensation targeted along the line between the two specific pads. Depending on your chosen frequency, you will observe mild, rhythmic, visible muscle twitching or complete sensory numbness in that isolated muscle zone.
